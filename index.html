TITLE: Detection of Fake And Clone Accounts in Twitter Using Classification and Distance Measure Algorithms

Published ID: JETNR2513244

Registration ID:JETNR_239490

Published on January 24, 2025
Authors: Eragani Pramodh, Arutla SaiRaj, Kammam Sai Akhil, Gujarathi Akhila, Gudimala Raju

ABSTARCT

The detection of fake and cloned accounts on social media platforms, particularly Twitter, has become increasingly important due to the potential harm these accounts can cause, such as spreading misinformation and undermining user trust. To effectively identify such accounts, various machine learning algorithms, including Decision Trees, Support Vector Machines, and K-Nearest Neighbors, can be employed to analyze user behavior patterns and account attributes. Additionally, similarity measures like Euclidean and Cosine similarity help quantify the differences between legitimate and fraudulent accounts. By integrating these techniques, a more accurate and comprehensive approach to safeguarding online environments can be established, ensuring the integrity of social media interactions.
